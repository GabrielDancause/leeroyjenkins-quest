Methodology

This study aggregates publicly available data from major esports tournament databases, including Esports Earnings, Liquipedia, and official developer announcements. We filtered for total prize money awarded up to the current date in 2026.

Note on exact figures: Prize pools are subject to currency fluctuations, crowdfunding variations (like Dota 2's Compendium), and unreported third-party events. The numbers represent the most accurate verified public data available but should be considered close estimates rather than absolute financial audits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which game has the biggest esports prize pool ever?
Dota 2 historically holds the record for the largest single tournament prize pool with The International 2021 (TI10) reaching over $40 million, largely driven by community crowdfunding through the in-game battle pass.
Who is the highest earning esports player?
The top earning esports players are predominantly Dota 2 professionals who have won The International, with players like N0tail surpassing $7 million in career tournament winnings.
Are mobile games big in esports?
Yes. Mobile titles like PUBG Mobile, Arena of Valor (Honor of Kings), and Mobile Legends: Bang Bang have massive prize pools that regularly rival or exceed traditional PC titles, largely driven by massive audiences in Asian markets.
How is esports prize money distributed?
Prize money is typically paid to the team organization first, which then takes a percentage cut (ranging from 0% to 20%) before distributing the remainder to the players and coaching staff according to their contracts.
Is the Fortnite World Cup still happening?
While the 2019 Fortnite World Cup featured a massive $30 million prize pool, Epic Games has since shifted to more regular, seasonal FNCS (Fortnite Champion Series) events rather than single mega-tournaments.
