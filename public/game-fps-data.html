Frequently Asked Questions

What FPS does RTX 4070 get in Valorant?
At 1080p Ultra settings, an RTX 4070 is estimated to achieve around 400 FPS in Valorant. At 1440p, it reaches about 262 FPS, and at 4K it still delivers a smooth 140 FPS. Because Valorant is highly CPU-bound, your actual framerate may vary depending on your processor.
Is RTX 4060 good for 1440p gaming?
The RTX 4060 is primarily a 1080p card, but it can handle 1440p in esports and older titles. For example, it gets ~133 FPS in Valorant and ~81 FPS in CS2 at 1440p. However, in heavy modern AAA games like Cyberpunk 2077 or Alan Wake 2, you can expect 30-40 FPS native at 1440p without DLSS.
Which GPU is best for Cyberpunk 2077?
Cyberpunk 2077 is one of the most demanding games available. For 4K Ultra gameplay, the RTX 4090 is the undisputed king, delivering ~35 FPS natively (though much higher with DLSS 3 Frame Generation). For 1440p, an RTX 4070 Ti or RX 7900 XT are excellent choices.
RTX 4070 vs RX 7800 XT which is faster?
The RX 7800 XT is generally slightly faster in pure rasterization (standard rendering) across most games. For instance, in Apex Legends at 1440p, the 7800 XT hits ~95 FPS vs the 4070's ~88 FPS. However, the RTX 4070 holds an advantage in ray tracing and features like DLSS 3.
What GPU do I need for 144 FPS?
It entirely depends on the game and resolution. For 144 FPS at 1080p in esports games (CS2, Valorant), an RX 7600 or RTX 4060 is more than enough. For 144 FPS at 1440p in AAA games (like Red Dead Redemption 2), you'll need at least an RTX 4080 or RX 7900 XTX.

Methodology

The performance data presented in this tool is estimated based on known GPU performance tiers and game engine demandingness scaling. While we strive for accuracy, these are algorithmic projections rather than direct hardware benchmarks of every specific combination.

Data was calculated using a performance index model where the RTX 4090 represents the baseline score (100). Games were categorized by their typical performance requirements (Esports, Older AAA, Modern AAA, Heavy AAA), and resolution penalties were applied using non-linear scaling to account for memory bandwidth and VRAM limitations at higher resolutions.

Note that at 1080p resolution, especially with high-end GPUs like the RTX 4090 and RTX 4080, performance in many games is heavily CPU-bottlenecked. The estimates provided attempt to account for these realistic engine and CPU caps.

All estimates assume "Ultra" or equivalent maximum visual settings, without the use of upscaling technologies like DLSS, FSR, or Frame Generation.
