Why Compatibility Matters

When building a PC, ensuring that all components are compatible and balanced is crucial. An unbalanced build can lead to bottlenecks, where one component limits the performance of others.

Power Consumption

Choosing the right Power Supply Unit (PSU) is very important. Always ensure your PSU provides enough wattage to cover the total power draw of your components with some headroom for efficiency and future upgrades.

FAQ

What is a bottleneck?

A bottleneck occurs when the capacity of a system or component is limited by a single slower component. In gaming PCs, this most commonly happens when a fast GPU is paired with a slow CPU, causing the GPU to wait for the CPU and reducing overall frame rates.

How much PSU wattage do I need?

As a rule of thumb, you should add up the total estimated wattage (TDP) of all components in your build, and choose a power supply that provides at least 25% to 50% more wattage than that number. This ensures efficiency, longevity, and leaves room for minor upgrades.
